We’re sorry, your 1 July payment may be a day late

We’re writing to let you know that due to our annual recalculation of minimum and maximum payments scheduled for 1 July 2021, your regular income stream payment which is due to be made that day, may be delayed until 2 July 2021.

We’re sorry for this potential delay and are currently working with our administrator to get your payment to you on time. If the delay becomes unavoidable, we can assure you that your payment won’t be more than one day late.

Option to reduce minimum income stream payments extended to 30 June 2022

Each year you must withdraw a minimum amount from your income stream. In response to investment market volatility and the COVID-19 pandemic, the Government temporarily halved these minimum payments from April 2020 to 30 June 2021.

On 29 May 2021, the Government proposed an extension of these rules. If this proposal goes ahead, you’ll be able to select the lower minimum payments until 30 June 2022.

For the latest news, visit the ATO website at ato.gov.au

You can change your payment amount or frequency

In July, we’ll write to you to ask you what income you’d like from your account for the next year. But don’t forget that you can update your payment amount or frequency at any time:

  • Online: Log into your online account
  • Over the phone: Call 1300 361 784

Once again, we’d like to apologise for the potential delay of your upcoming payment and any inconvenience this may cause.
