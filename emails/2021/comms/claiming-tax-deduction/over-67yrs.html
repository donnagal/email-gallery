We’re writing to let you know that you may be able to claim a tax deduction on the personal contributions you’ve made to your super account between 1 July 2020 and 30 June 2021.

All you need to do is:

1. Download

Download the Claim a tax deduction for personal contributions to super fact sheet.

 

2. Fill in

Read it carefully, and fill in the form if you decide to claim

 

3. Send

Send it back to us here at Cbus by replying to this email.

 

Once you receive a response from us, you can lodge your tax return.

If you’ve already sent us a copy of the ATO Notice of intent to claim or vary a deduction for personal super contribution form, we’ll use that form instead. You don’t need to apply again.

To double check the total amount of personal contributions you’ve made this year, simply log on to member online or give us a call on 1300 361 784 (8am to 8pm AEST weekdays).

If you’ve withdrawn money from your super

You may not be able to claim the full amount of a tax deduction on personal contributions if you’ve taken money out of your account. For example, if you’ve received a COVID-19 early release payment, opened an income stream or rolled over to another fund. So if you’ve withdrawn any money from your account, make sure you give us a call before you submit your application to check if you’re eligible to apply.

If you're planning to keep contributing in 2021/22

If you want to continue to put money into your Cbus super account there are some requirements you need to meet. The Voluntary contributions work test confirmationVoluntary contributions work test confirmation form explains what these are, so if you want to keep making contributions, make sure you complete this form and send it back to us.

More information

Tax rules are complex and can vary based on your individual circumstances. It's important you're familiar with the rules that apply to tax deductions for personal super contributions.
