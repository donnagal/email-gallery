What is a Self Managed Super Fund (SMSF)? Self Managed Super Funds are an alternative to APRA-regulated super funds run by financial institutions, employers and industry funds like Cbus.

SMSFs are typically family or one-member funds set up to give individuals direct control of their super investment. SMSF members are also trustees, which means they are legally responsible for fund decisions and for complying with super and tax laws.

Like other super funds, SMSFs can generally only be used to provide for retirement. But they are quite different in the way they are set up, managed and regulated.

Questions you need to ask
To help decide whether an SMSF is right for you, consider;

  • Do you have enough time?
  • Do you have the knowledge and experience?
  • Do you have enough super?
  • Will you get a better result?
  • Can you afford it?
