Dangerous Occupation Exception

It is a year since a policy to protect workers in hazardous occupations has been in operation. Just for the building and construction sector that Cbus covers, the Dangerous Occupation Exception has allowed 115,800 building and construction workers to obtain or retain vital insurance.

Cbus is standing up for members in the construction industry

The Government wants to change super laws that could see workers new to the industry ‘stapled’ to a fund that won’t cover them for dangerous occupations. 'Stapling' means someone will stay in their first fund, unless they choose another fund and risks leaving workers in hazardous occupations without critical insurance cover. Cbus is concerned that the availability of insurance for workers in hazardous occupations is at risk of being undermined with the proposals in the Your Future, Your Super Bill.
