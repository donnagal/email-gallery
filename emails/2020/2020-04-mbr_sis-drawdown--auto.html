Temporary reduction to minimum income payments

The law requires you to draw a minimum income from your account each year. As a result of recent investment market volatility and the COVID-19 pandemic, some members may have seen a drop in the value of their account. To address this, the minimum has been temporarily halved by the Government.

You set up your account just after these changes were introduced. So, if you selected the minimum payment option on your application form (that’s also the default option), your payments will be in line with the new reduced minimum amounts shown below:

 
Your age Reduced minimum payment Standard minimum payment
Under 65 2% 4%
65 to 74 2.5% 5%
75 to 79 3% 6%
80 to 84 3.5% 7%
85 to 89 4.5% 9%
90 to 94 5.5% 11%
95 and over 7% 14%
 

This means your regular payments could be lower than you expected when you joined. For example, if you’re currently age 65, the standard minimum payment is 5% of your account balance each year. However, this has temporarily been halved and is now just 2.5%.

 

The reduced minimum payments apply for the:

  • current financial year ending 30 June 2020
  • next financial year ending 30 June 2021.

Payments will return to the standard minimum payments from
1 July 2021.

 

Check your income payments are right for you

It’s a good idea to check your regular income payments are still right for you.
