The Coronavirus (COVID-19) has created a unique situation and many of our members feel concerned about its impact.

Regular updates are being published online but here are a couple of important considerations that may assist during this difficult time:

 

• Option to reduce minimum income stream payments 

• Re-assess Centrelink Age Pension entitlements

 

Option to reduce minimum income stream payments

 

The law requires you to draw a minimum payment from your account each year. As a result of investment market volatility, this minimum has been halved for the rest of the current financial year and next financial year ending 30 June 2021.

Your age New minimum payment
Under 65 2%
65 to 74 2.5%
75 to 79 3%
80 to 84 3.5%
85 to 89 4.5%
90 to 94 5.5%
95 and over 7%

 

Now, we understand that not everyone can afford to reduce their payments. But if you can, this may provide your savings more time to recover any loss of value that you may have experienced recently.

How to change your income payments:

•

Online: Log into your online account  

•

Over the phone: Call 1300 361 784

Re-assess Centrelink Age Pension entitlements

Centrelink works out if you qualify for the Age Pension and how much you receive. One of the factors they use is your income stream account balance, and a lower balance might result in an increase to Age Pension entitlements for some. 

 

How to have your Age Pension re-assessed

Step 1: Get an updated Centrelink Schedule from Cbus:

•

Online: Log into your online account  

•

Over the phone: Call 1300 361 784
 
We're experiencing higher than normal call volumes. Please go online to avoid lengthy delays if you can. 		  

Step 2: Lodge the updated Schedule with Centrelink:

•

Log into your myGov account and select Centrelink  

•

Follow the instructions to load your updated schedule
