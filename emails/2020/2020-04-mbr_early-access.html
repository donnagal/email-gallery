Many of you are aware that the Government is allowing affected individuals to early access up to $10,000 of their superannuation before 1 July 2020 as part of the response to COVID 19. They will also be able to access up to a further $10,000 from 1 July 2020 until 24 September 2020. There are clear eligibility requirements that must be met to access your funds, this ATO page about early access to your super provides further details.

Cbus is supportive of the practical assistance the scheme will be to members who are facing financial stress during this challenging period. We have made preparations to make this as quick as possible. From receipt of approved files from the ATO we anticipate payment to your bank account within 5-10 working days.

For those members who are still considering their position and would like more information, the Early access table below can give you and idea of the potential impacts to your retirement lump sum.

Assumptions

1. Withdrawing from your account $10,000 April 20th - July 1st 2020 and $10,000 July to Sept 2020.

2. Retirement balances are presented in future dollars.

3. Assume default Death &TPD insurance premiums for Manual worker.

4. Admin fee of 0.19% p.a. and $104 p.a. (inflating at CPI) apply.

5. Assume net of tax investment return of 5.75% p.a. under the default MySuper Growth option.

6. Assume long term CPI of 2.5% p.a. and rise in living standard of 1% p.a.

7. Assumes retirement at age 67.

From 20 April 2020, the ATO will be receiving applications through the myGov website.

There are currently COVID-19 (Coronavirus) themed phishing emails and SMSs in circulation. If you are approached about paying a fee to release your super, it is likely to be a scam. The Australian Taxation Office is managing all claims for the early release of super through the myGov online service. There is no need to involve a third party or pay a fee to get access under this scheme.
